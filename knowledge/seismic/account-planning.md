Introduction
In the world of sales and marketing, account planning and territory planning play a crucial
role in identifying growth opportunities and maximizing revenue. Account planning involves
strategically managing important accounts by understanding customer needs and creating
value for both parties. On the other hand, territory planning focuses on dividing the market
into manageable segments to allocate resources effectively and identify growth
opportunities. By implementing best practices in these areas, organizations can drive
growth and revenue effectively.

Territory planning
Territory planning is equally vital in maximizing sales efficiency and coverage. It involves
dividing the market into manageable segments to allocate resources effectively and identify
growth opportunities. To develop an effective territory plan, it is crucial to analyze the
market potential, assess the competition, and understand the geographic and demographic
aspects of the territory.
In territory planning, it is essential to look for areas with high growth potential, untapped
markets, and emerging trends.
What are some factors to consider when developing an effective territory plan?

Prioritization
You need to prioritize the accounts that have the biggest chance of generating revenue.
It's much easier to expand than land a new account, which is why you want to prioritize the
Accounts with the most renewable revenue, then go into those with the most potential.
Once you identify your top accounts based on the prior research, that's when you should
progress into building an Account Plan for those specific accounts.

Account planning
What is Account Planning?
Account planning involves a strategic approach to managing and growing a company's most
important accounts.
Account Planning is essential to understand the customer's needs and align them with the
organization's objectives, creating value for both parties. By identifying key stakeholders,
understanding their challenges, and offering tailored solutions, account planning aims to
build strong, long-term relationships with clients.
When it comes to account planning, some best practices include conducting thorough
research on the target accounts, understanding their industry, and creating personalized
account plans to address their unique requirements. Utilizing customer relationship
management (CRM) tools can also streamline the process by capturing important data,
tracking interactions, and managing tasks related to each account.
What is the goal of account planning?
The goal of account planning is to understand the customer's needs and align them with the
organization's objectives, creating value for both parties and building strong, long-term
relationships with clients.

Negative Account Signs
Negative Signs: There are certain signs in an account that things are NOT going well,
which means it may not be wise to spend too much of your prospecting efforts. Some of
these signs can include:
• large scale layoffs
• researchers not knowing what projects they are currently focusing on or what is next
for them
• in person - are people actually there working?
• poor stock performance

Positive Account Signs
Positive Account Signs: There are certain signs in an account that things ARE going well,
which means it may be wise to spend more of your time, prospecting and efforts into these
accounts. Some of these signs can include:
• recent funding
• company hiring relevant roles
• positive results in early stage clinical programs
• most companies reaching out with inbound requests
• strong stock performance

Execution and conclusion
Proper execution of account and territory planning involves clear communication,
collaboration between AE, SDR and marketing teams, and utilizing data-driven insights.
Regular reviews and adjustments to the plans based on performance and market dynamics
are also necessary for long-term success.
In conclusion, account planning and territory planning are integral parts of a successful
sales strategy. By understanding the importance of these practices and implementing best
practices, organizations can effectively identify top accounts, allocate resources efficiently,
and ultimately drive growth and revenue.
