Overview
Clinical PBMC
Establishing and maintaining a clinical site requires lots of experience and great project
management skills. A good management team needs to start planning a clinical trial months
ahead. Their actions could even precede the budgeting phase. With many things in their mind,
the site managers would appreciate every chance to offload some of the work, such as blood
sample processing and related logistics (comparatively less essential, but demanding enough
to feel troublesome) to others. This is where we can help to make a difference.
As a service vendor, we must learn to cope with the uncertainties and navigate with foresights
and flexibility, while embracing a rather long sales cycle. As an example, it is not uncommon
to feel the challenge of needing to issue a quote before a customer can provide essential
details of the project. However, from the customers perspective, pricing is an indispensable
piece of info for budgetary purposes. Therefore, clear communications will help to create a
perception of professionalism and hassle-free partnering experience.
Last but not least, make it a mission to follow up with the lead on regular basis. The more we
show up on a client's radar, the more likely that we will stay in the game.

Offerings
A clinical opportunity is almost always highly custom. As a rule of thumb, loop in our SMEs
for visibility and collaboration. Consider using the following talk points to increase our
chances.
• Custom kitting
We have well-established supply chain management at our Woburn, MA site. Our
specialists at this site routinely maintain an inventory of raw materials, assemble
phlebotomy kits (from shipping container, butterfly needles, blood tubes, to gloves and
absorbent pads), and dispatch these kits on-demand to various locations with precision
and timeliness. This capability was originally built for our translational studies, but can be
leveraged to add more values (i.e. running stock rooms, additional procurement and
staffing) to a clinical program.
• On-site blood and matched sample collection
Clinical sites usually prefer to own the blood collections, but we may come across
temporary sites that are looking to hire this capability. We have an extensive pool of
phlebotomists that can be stationed to these sites based on a set cadence, or on-demand,
provided arrangements are made with sufficient lead time.
Additionally, we can help to collect other minimally invasive samples (i.e. urine, stool, saliva
and swabs) along with the blood collections, if the clinical studies call for them.
• Courier
Our clients may prefer to use their courier of choice, which we will accommodate. We use
standard FedEx overnight service for all clinical samples unless specified otherwise. It is

This is our core capability and strength. Our service lab opens 6 days (Mon- Sat) a week.

We also provide short-term and long-term storage of samples at -20°C, -80°C or liquid
nitrogen following industry standards. We will batch samples and ship them at regular
intervals or upon request by a client.
• Customizations
More often than not, clinical programs must follow a set of pre-approved SOPs that may
differ from Sanguine's SOP, or the SOPs of any service vendor they choose. Variations are
seen not only in procedures, but also in the choice of raw material, consumables, reagents,
equipment as well as logistics (i.e. PBMC isolation must be done within 4 hours of blood
draw, or FedEx must be used instead of UPS or World Courier, or aliquoting plan). We are
more nimble than competitors at accommodating those requirements to meet the rigor of
clinical regulations. Bring SMEs to your communications to make sure thorough
assessment is made.

Value
Pharmaceutical companies often need to establish clinical sites, or collaborate with existing
clinical sites, for a number of reasons including:
• Clinical trial - A mandatory step during drug development that requires controlled
environment to thoroughly test the new drug in human.
• Patient access - Clinical trials require access to a diverse patient population
representing the demographics and medical conditions for which a new drug or
treatment is intended.
• Expertise and infrastructure - Clinical sites often have specialized expertise,
infrastructure, and resources necessary for conducting clinical trials, such as
experienced investigators, clinical research coordinators, laboratory facilities, and
access to medical and research equipment.
• Regulatory compliance - Regulatory bodies enforces strict rules throughout clinical
trials. Clinical sites play a crucial role in ensuring compliance by following SOPs that
encompasses all essential practices. These requirements is relayed to partners if
external services is recruited.
• Efficiency, timeliness and cost control - Acquisition of all capabilities, infrastructure
and expertise requires substantial prep work, followed by years of practices and
refining. Outsourcing peripheral (as opposed to core) needs makes logistical and
financial sense in many cases.

site managers. With our clinical sample processing offerings, Sanguine is able to provide
some alleviations. Refer to the following list of our value propositions:

Custom Protocols and Training
Regulation surrounding clinical trials is very stringent, ensuring the safety and efficacy of
new medical interventions before they reach the market. Full documentation must be
submitted to FDA along with IND or NDA filing. To Sanguine, we will be asked for
documents such as experimental procedures and results (may need to use clients'
templates), but could expand to certification of the service lab, technician training &
qualification, device maintenance record, etc. In many cases, we will also be asked to
follow client-provided protocols to help lessen the administrative burden on their end.
At Sanguine, we are able to provide PBMC/serum/plasma isolation services for clinical
trials, while meeting the compliance requirements mentioned above. Most clinical PBMC
processing protocols are very similar to our SOP. However, custom protocols are seldomly
identical to ours. Some common deviations include but are not limited to:
• Maximum allowed processing delay from time of collection
• Centrifugation speed and time
• Blood sample dilution and wash number
• Key reagents (i.e. Ficolll vs. LymphoPrep, CryoStor vs. Gibco Cryopreservation
Medium, etc.)
• Key consumable (i.e. SepMate tube vs. LeucoSep tube)
In certain cases, our customer may offer to provide all reagents and consumables (in
addition to clinical samples) in order to control the manufacturer, catalog & lot number of
those items. On our end, we need to assess the safety, storage, usage tracking, disposal of
these materials as well as the cost of managing the changes.
In addition, the manpower required for a clinical trial program should never be

underestimated. To build in the necessary redundancy for a project that may run from
months to years, our client would usually request at least 2-3 lab technicians trained and
certified to execute the custom protocol. We need to allocate technician time to arrange
the training sessions that would compete with their routine assignments, in addition to the
time and resources needed for logistics. Once certified, technician time would be allocated
for every incoming delivery. To avoid human errors, we do not (and will be asked to not to)
mix up samples from different master plans. To account for the commitment of our time,
good coordination is a must and we will also build in a no-show charge to encourage the
customer to abide by their arrangements.
In the light of the complexity of a clinical trial opportunity and potential risk, it is mandatory
to involve other teams in the evaluation and pricing phase. Start a group chat or a channel
on Slack to keep all communications centralized.

Clinical PBMCs range from $600-$2000 a sample - the average being ~$1200. This really
depends on the following items referenced on the previous page, including:
• How many samples / sample types
• Custom processing
• Custom reagents
• Timeline
• Shipping details
• Training requirements
