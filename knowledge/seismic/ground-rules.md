Ground Rules Overview
Ground Rules
Ground rules are a tool that helps you frame your conversations so everyone knows what’s
going to happen, there aren’t any surprises, and it’s easier to set a clear next step at the
end of your meetings instead of spinning your wheels chasing prospects.
The key elements to a setting effective grounds rules are:
• Time: How long is the meeting?
• The Prospect’s Top 3: What are the top things they want to talk about?
• Your Top 3 : What are the top three things you want to talk about?
• Outcomes: Every meeting should end with either a “Yes”, “No”, or “Next Step”.
It’s important that you use all four components of the ground rules in the order that it’s
outlined. However, feel free to tailor how you deliver the four components so it sounds
natural.

When to use Ground Rules
When to use Ground Rules
You can set ground rules in every interaction you have with a prospect, client, or colleague,
however, here are some of the most common:
• Cold call
• Lead Qualification Call (SDR)
• Discovery call
• Capabilities Presentation
• Requirements Gathering Call
• Proposal Meeting
• Kick-Off Call
• Check-In Call
• Annual/Quarterly Business Review

Time
Time
Discussing the time on ground rules can be one that is easily overlooked, but it's also very
important. Let's say we skip it and lose track of time, the client cuts you off because there
is a hard stop, you don't have time to set next steps and then the client ghosts you.
Perhaps you go over the allotted time and the client gets upset you're not respecting their
time.
Or the client shows up 15 minutes into a 30 minute meeting, and you might not have
enough time to cover what you want.
How do we handle these situations?
As always, you should always personalize your ground rules to: Your sales style, your
prospect and the situation. Here are some additional examples:
• "Thank you for taking the time to join today. It's been one of those days that are back
to back - are you all good to go until the top of the hour?"
• Client shows up late: "Totally understand. It looks like we have about 15 minutes so we
can try and cover as much as we can. Are you able to go past X time, or should we
reschedule so we have more time to discuss XYZ?"

Opp Qualification
Opp Qualification Call Examples
AE: I’m glad everyone was able to make our call today. I know we have __________ minutes
set aside for our meeting. Does that still work for everyone?
Prospect: Yes, that works.
AE: I understand you’re interested in ___________ for an upcoming study so we’ll definitely
discuss that. Are there any specific questions or topics you know you want to cover during
our time today?
Prospect: Responds
AE: Great, we’ll definitely be able to touch base on those. I’d like to get a better
understanding of exactly what you’re looking for as well as your timeline and budget so I’ll
be asking you a few questions.
At the end of our call we can discuss any potential next steps so that we’re all on the same
page.
How does that sound?

Requirements Gathering
Requirements Gathering
AE: I’m glad everyone was able to make our call today. I know we have __________ minutes
set aside for our meeting. Does that still work for everyone?
Prospect: Yes, that works. *
AE: Last time we talked you shared a lot of information about your study and what you all
were looking for, but we didn’t get a chance to dive deep into your specific requirements
which is what we’re going to focus on today.
I figured we’d do a brief recap of what you all shared last time, check in to see if anything
has changed, and then walk through your requirements so we have as much information as
possible to design your study.
At the end of our call, I will walk you through what’s going to be happening next so you
know exactly what to expect.
Does that work for everyone?
Prospect: Sounds good.

Proposal Call
Proposal Call Example
AE: I’m glad everyone was able to make our call today. I know we have __________ minutes
set aside for our meeting. Does that still work for everyone?
Prospect: Yes, that works.
AE: Has anything changed since the last time we talked?
*This is my favorite question to ask, especially before a proposal call where they may have
changed their criteria, timeline etc.
Prospect: Yes/No
AE: Okay. Last time we talked we discussed (quickly mentioned what was discussed).
Today we’re going to walk through our proposal and how we can help you (mention what
their goals are), review the timeline and pricing for this project. Then, we can review next
steps and see if it makes sense to move forward or not. Just a heads up, I’d like this to be
a conversation, not a presentation, so feel free to ask questions as they come up.
Does that work?
Prospect: That works.
*When you dive into the Proposal call, you always want to remind them of the problem you
are trying to solve.

Kickoff Call Example

3. Walk you through the process and next steps

to use this time to confirm all of your requirements, get the right (blackout dates {if relevant
- same day or next day shipping studies not lab}, delivery information etc). Anything else
you'd all like to review?

Check-In Calls
Check-In Call Example
AE: I’m glad everyone was able to make our call today. I know we have __________ minutes
set aside for our meeting. Does that still work for everyone?
Client: Yes, that works.
AE: We’re halfway through your study so today we’re going to do a midpoint check-in to
make sure everything is on track. I know you all mentioned you all wanted to talk about
(anything they sent you in advance). (If applicable) We’ll also walk you through a couple of
ideas that we think would optimize your study or other capabilities you might consider in
the future.
* In advance of the check-in call you want to ask them if they have any specific things they
want to discuss during the call. Insert whatever is relevant to the study.
Does that work for everyone?
Client: Yes
AE: Great and as always we’ll discuss any to-dos or next steps at the end.
