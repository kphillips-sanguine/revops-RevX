Inbound Steps to Follow
1. Forward inbound email notification to Jack, Chelsea, and Marketing@sanguinebio.com,
saying “On It”
2. Within Salesforce, bring up the lead profile and update lead ownership to yourself.
3. Within Gong, add lead to a dedicated inbound flow to ensure proper lead status
update and follow-up notifications.
• Without adding a lead to a flow, the lead status sits as an MQL, which indicated it
is not being worked.
• Minimum of 4 emails, LinkedIn connection, phone call, and 2 LinkedIn messages
built within inbound flow required.
◦If the phone number is not readily available, ask Jack to look it up for you.
4. Once Outreach is performed, confirm the lead is added to the “Marketing Leads
(Handraisers- Hubspot)” tab of the Marketing Leads Sheet. *This process is automated,
but if data is missing inform Eliza.
• Update Columns P through V with related information and continually check back
on the Marketing Leads sheet with relevant Updates.
5. Once the lead is finished being worked, regardless of outcome, update columns T
through V with relevant outcomes.

Inbound Lead Expectations
• Every Inbound lead is worked on within 1 hour of form submission. 15 minutes if not in
an active meeting or working on an urgent task.
• Every Inbound lead is added to a Gong Engage flow to ensure status is updated
properly. -Ex: Flow structure here
• Every Inbound lead is worked thoroughly based on the flow steps above to ensure we
make the most of every website submission.
• The Marketing Leads sheet is updated within a reasonable timeframe and kept up-to-
date weekly with relevant outcomes. -Recommendation! Block 30 minutes on your
calendar on Friday afternoons to update the marketing sheet!
• Any potential good-fit lead that does not convert is added into a long-term nurture flow
and contacted accordingly.
• Leads that never respond to email, LinkedIn, or phone call should be marked as such
based on the status/outcome breakdown below, and Marketing will put them back into
a long-term nurture campaign

Stage/Outcome Reference Guide
• Meeting: Meeting has taken place, and discussions are ongoing
• Opportunity: Opportunity has been created, lead converted to contact
• Disqualified- Highly Unfeasible: Request was fully outside of Sanguine’s Capabilities,
ie, Disease population inaccessable or sample type not either feasible or in progress
for product expansion.
• No Response: After completing the full inbound flow outlined above, the lead never
engaged.
• Working: Either active outreach or conversation pre-meeting is ongoing
• Disqualified- Could be feasible: Feasible request lead did not want to move forward
with. Ie. Timeline, budget, etc.
◦Reasoning should be added to the “Disqualified Reason” column of the Marketing
leads document.
• Lost - Inventory Gap: Lead only interested in inventory samples, which were not
available, and/or MTO not of interest.
• QNO: Qualified lead, not ready to move forward, and in active nurture flow
