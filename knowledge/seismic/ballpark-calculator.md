Ballparking
*If you haven't completed the Qualification Lesson, please do that prior to taking this
training.
Trying to understand BUDGET is a huge part of the qualification process for prospective
collections. If they are looking for inventory pricing on prospective collections, we are not
going to be the best fit, as Sanguine's services offer well more than just the sample itself.
That is why giving a ballpark range can help qualify if we are a fit or not. However, there are
some risks with ballparking:
If you ballpark too high, you may scare them off.
If you ballpark too low, then come back higher, your client may feel misled or may
not have the appropriate budget.
And that is exactly why Sanguine developed the ballpark calculator to assist.

Utilizing the calculator
First of all, always utilize the most up-to-date Ballpark Calculator. You can find this by
going into the Sanguine Resources Collection and clicking the Ballpark Calculator Link.
You should download the version you are working on, that way if another person is also
utilizing it you aren't calculating the wrong items.
There are many tabs to help you with various aspects of ballparking. Let's break them
down starting with the Calculator:
Calculator Tab
The Calculator Tab is best utilized when you know the study specifics and can input
them directly to give a more accurate price. Fill in subject fees PER subject, not the
total study needs.
1. All new studies will have a study startup fee (select yes)
2. If a study requires a new study protocol/ICF, select yes. Please reference the IRB
section of the Pricing Calculator because other fees may apply such as subject facing
materials etc
3. NPO partnership required - if yes, there are additional startup fees that apply
4. All studies will have project management - if this is a lab opp, this will slightly
increase.
5. Kitting - all studies have a standard kitting fee. From there, you also need to select the
types of tubes required - i.e. basic blood or specialty such as CPT or PaxGene
a. If there is custom kitting, you can manually enter the cost of supplies (do this per

PID Timelines
Timelines are also a huge part of qualification and ballparking. As a reminder, we build our
timelines based on the Tier and the prevalence. We add 1-2 months for startup on top of
these timelines which are highlighted in the notes.
Prior to giving our timelines, always remember to ask if they have an end date in mind to
receive these samples.

Ranges
The Ranges tab is to help you give some quick figures when you are on a call and don't
know all of the study details but need to provide a ballpark. You should know these ranges
based on Tier but this is a handy tool to have on deck.
As you're learning from this training, you should provide a RANGE. Especially if there are
other elements such as in home processing (can add $310+ depending on number of
aliquots and spins). Go a little under, and a little over depending on those numbers!
As a reminder these are built on the assumption of 10 donors. If there are more than
10 donors, the cost per sample will be less. If there are fewer, it will be more!

Lab
Lab is a little bit trickier to ballpark because you will need to know the total number of
tubes going into the lab to appropriately get the price.
To break it down, if you have 100mL of whole blood in 10mL tubes, you will have 10 tubes
that need to be accessioned. For PBMCs, since it's a 1:1 ratio, you will also have 10 tubes
for Sample Retrieval. For Plasma since its a 2:1 ratio, there will only be 5 tubes for Sample
Retrieval. For storage, it will be the number of tubes x number of months.
Follow the sample instructions or ask Feasibility for assistance.
UCLA has hidden fees other than the test. These are broken down on the UCLA testing
Calculator but you can also refer to the pricebook as needed.
