Fundamentals
3 main types of joints
• Fibrous Joints - skull sutures
• Cartilaginous Joint - intervertebral discs
• Synovial Joints - Shoulder, elbow, knee, hip joints (ball and socket, hinge, pivot)
What Is Synovial Fluid?
• A viscous, fluid found in joint cavities.
• Function: Lubricates joints, reduces friction, nourishes cartilage.
Where Is It Found?
• Located in synovial joints (e.g., knees, elbows, shoulders).
• Produced by the synovial membrane (Synovium) lining the joint capsule.

In people with rheumatic diseases like rheumatoid arthritis, their immune system mistakenly
attacks the joints. This causes inflammation in the joint lining (synovium), which leads to:

---

<!-- Page 5 -->
1. Increased Fluid Production
The inflamed synovium produces more synovial fluid than normal in response to the
irritation.
2. Immune Cells Enter the Joint
White blood cells and inflammatory proteins build up in the joint, making the fluid
thicker and more abundant.
3. Poor Drainage
The body’s normal process for draining fluid is disrupted, so the fluid accumulates in
the joint space
The Result:
Joints become swollen, stiff, and painful due to the extra fluid and inflammation —
which is why synovial fluid samples from these patients are valuable for studying
autoimmune and joint diseases.

Processing & Storage Options Clients May Ask For
• Additives:
◦Anticoagulation: If clotting is a concern or cell counts are required, use an
anticoagulant like EDTA or heparin.
◦Hyaluronidase Treatment: For highly viscous samples or when performing
proteomic analysis, consider treating the fluid with hyaluronidase.
◦RNAlater - for RNA analysis
• Processing:
◦Centrifugation: If you are performing metabolomic analysis, centrifugation before
freezing helps remove cells and cellular debris.
◦SF can also be sent without centrifuging

---

<!-- Page 19 -->
◦1mL is our standard aliquot size
• Preservation:
◦DMSO: at 5-10% is beneficial for preserving cellular morphology when freezing
synovial fluid.
◦Snap Freezing (not currently offered - working with sites to see if possible): For
optimal metabolomic analysis, snap freezing with liquid nitrogen is the
recommended freezing method
◦Controlled Rate Freezing - our standard
