Importance of Next Steps
Next Steps
Trying to drive next steps in a deal is crucial for having it move forward. If you aren't able to
get next steps, your deal is going to stall out which can impact forecast and/or win rate.
We are going to talk about some tips for helping generate next steps and add "urgency" to
your deals.

---

<!-- Page 3 -->
Ground Rules
In your ground rules, you always want to say you will discuss next steps at the end.
It's helpful to give them an out. How do you do this? Very simply. "We can save a few
minutes at the end to discuss if it makes sense to continue conversations, or not"
By mentioning this at the beginning of your call, you are preparing your prospect or client
that this next step conversation will occur.

---

<!-- Page 4 -->
At the end of a call
When you start discussing next steps at the end of a call, it is really important to ask
general questions (think top of the funnel) and then get more granular. Here are some
examples:
Capabilities Calls
• Yes - Would it make sense to put another time on the calendar to discuss that project
and see if it makes sense to partner with Sanguine or not?
• No - When would be a good time for me to follow up with you to see if anything has
changed?
◦If they give you a time, say you'll put a placeholder on the calendar as a reminder
to check in
◦If they don't, say you will follow up in a month or two to keep them updated on
Sanguine's changing capabilities and see if they have any new projects
Now that you have a better understanding of our needs, are there any current
projects you have a need for samples for?
Study Design Calls
Now that we have all the information we need on our side, we will take this back
internally to review and work on generating a proposal for you. It'll probably take
about a week to schedule. Do you want to put time on X day to walk through the
pricing together?"

Proposal Calls
"Since this proposal is around what you were expecting, what does it look like to move this
forward on your side?
• Depending on what they say, ask for timelines and try and schedule that solid next
step
"We have to take it up to management"
"Completely understand. Do you have a withstanding meeting with them to review things
like this, or when do you plan to have that regroup?"
"Next week."
"Great. Would it make sense to throw some time on the calendar after that meeting to
check-in and see if it makes sense to keep moving forward or not?"
Getting a solidified next meeting is a crucial next step. But it's important to have a
REASON for that meeting.

Adding Urgency
You're trying to generate next steps, but the client is dragging their feet. How do you get
them to move faster?
There are a few ways to add urgency to your deal, but it will depend on the client, the
persona, and the urgency of the deal.
For example, the "Working Backwards" method is always effective. I"f you need all these
samples by X date, we need to sign by Y." However, it doesn't always work out that way.
Let's talk about a few other options.

---

<!-- Page 7 -->
Expiration
Utilize Proposal Expiration Dates
Hi X,
Just wanted to reach out and let you know the quote I provided back in *date* is set to
expire at the end of this month and we have had some recent price increases.
I know your team is still working on *reason: ex the assays in preparation for this project*,
but it would be great to get a sense of the current timeline you are expecting to get this
started up to lock you into these prices.
*Be cautious when you mention price increase: Because if they don't budge, and then you
don't increase price, they will call your bluff in the future.
Another way you can try this route could be:
Hi X,
Just wanted to reach out and let you know the quote I provided back in *date* is set to
expire at the end of this month.
I know your team is still working on *reason: ex the assays in preparation for this project*,
but it would be great to get a sense of the current timeline you are expecting to see if we
need to revise the proposal. Do you think this will move forward prior to the expiration?

EOQ
End of Quarter, End of Year
Tip: Use internal resources as your reason.
Being the end of quarter, we want to make sure this study gets into the cue and gets
kicked off in order to capture *insert relevant to study ex: the respiratory infection patients
during the peak time of year.* With the 30 day startup period, if we were to get the signed
document and PO this week, we would be looking to launch collections the first week of
*date*
Otherwise, it may be delayed to get kicked-off and we really want to help you hit your
timeline (or add some other value!)

Recruitment Launches
Recruitment Launches:
For hard to reach populations, or a really specific I/E, use recruitment as a way to add
urgency.
• We have already run some preliminary outreach campaigns to our *Indication patients*
and identified some patients, but it would be very helpful to either expedite the launch
of this project or at least provide some further guidelines about timelines so that we
can keep our donors warm and ready to participate. Do you think you will be able to
launch (by X date, this week...)?
Priority IF timeline is important, and a high demand item (disease leukos):
• We have another client that is due to launch *Indication* leukopak collections soon. As
I mentioned, we have already contacted our HS database and have identified a few
interested donors.
However, if this client launches the project prior to yours, then priority will be given to them.
I know your team has been speaking to Sanguine/us for a while about this request and that
timeline is very important, so I am asking for an update to see if we can maintain *their
company* priority with these donors. Do you have a few minutes to meet and discuss next
steps on your end?
The more questions you can ask to drive next steps and urgency, the better.

Conclusion
You never want to leave a call and just say, OK thank you for chatting, bye! You want to
make sure you're leaving that call knowing:
• When will you speak with the client again?
• What is the follow up plan?
• What are you going to cover in your next meeting?
• What is holding this deal up?
You can try and add urgency by using some tactics like:
• Clients timeline
• Expiration dates
• Backlog / Internal resources
• Recruitment
