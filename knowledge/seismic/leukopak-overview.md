Overview
Leukopak Definition
• A leukopak is an enriched apheresis product collected via leukapheresis, the act of
extracting white blood cells (WBCs) from the peripheral blood. They contain a high
concentration of peripheral blood mononuclear cells (PBMCs) – which consists of T cells,
B cells, NK cells, and monocytes. These products can contain > 1E10 (10 Billion) cells per
donor
Leukopak Overview
• Leukopaks contain a high concentration of peripheral blood mononuclear cells
(PBMCs) – which consists of T cells, B cells, NK cells, and monocytes. These products
can contain > 1E10 (10 Billion) cells per donor
Leukopak Collections:
• Leukopaks are collected at an apheresis collection site, and must be done under the
supervision of a registered nurse
• The leukapheresis procedure takes ~3-4 hours for each collection, and is performed on
a Spectra Optia collection device
• During leukapheresis, the Optia device acts as a giant "centrifuge," removing white
blood cells from the donor's whole blood and returning the other blood components
back to the donor (red blood cells, plasma, platelets, etc.)

Value
Healthy & Disease State Leukopaks:
• More Cells per donor: Leukapheresis allows for up to 10 billion+ white blood cells
collected per donation
• Isolation of rare cell subtypes: Higher quantity of cells collected per donor allow for the
isolation of more rare cell types (e.g. CD34 cells, gamma-delta T cells)
• Assay Validation: High quantity of cells per donor allows for reproducibility in
experimentation– same donor collected at the same time = same results in a given
assay
• Recallability: All of Sanguine's apheresis donors are recallable
• Longitudinal Sampling: Ability to schedule multiple collections across a period of time
to help researchers monitor disease progression
• Cost Effectiveness: price of cells per mL of product is much lower than whole blood or
other sample types
Disease State Only:
• Medical Records : EHR integration allows for robust clinical history as a data
deliverable
• "Mimicking the Clinical Product": Cell therapy companies working on therapies for a
given condition can order disease state leukopaks to "practice" for what they will
receive in their eventual clinical trial
• 40+ Conditions: Sanguine is the market leader in disease state leukopak collections,
and can offer 10 times the number of conditions on average vs our competitors
* One of Sanguine's key differentiators in our product portfolio is disease state leukopaks*

Screening & Collection Visits
Screening & Collection Visits
Leukopak collections occur in a two visit approach for both disease state and healthy
collections.
1. Screening Visit
• First, donors are recruited remotely by Sanguine research coordinators and consented
remotely. If needed, medical records are pulled. This mirrors our translational process
exactly
• Next, donors are sent to either the LA site or a partner apheresis site for a screening
visit. During this visit, a health history questionnaire is filled out (HHQ), the donor
undergoes a physical assessment by the nurse, and blood tubes are drawn for CBC
and Infectious disease testing.
• The screening visit usually takes about 1 hour and requires the donor to travel to the
site
2. Collection visit
• Once the donor's CBC and IDT results are received from our partner lab (results
received usually ~1 week post screening visit), Labcorp, then the donor is scheduled
for a collection visit.
• The collection visit is scheduled anywhere from 1 week–30 days after the screening
visit occurs, and is dependent on both client and donor availability
• The collection visit is where the Leukopak product is collected in a process that takes
about 3-4 hours

validated for 2-8 degrees Celsius, and delivered directly to the client or shipped to

• TNC counts are generated on the same day of collection
• CoAs are generated within 24 hours of collection

Leukopak Product Variants
Leukopak Splitting:
• Sanguine has purchased the Terumo SCD IIB Sterile welder machine to allow for the
splitting of fresh leukopaks
• This machine is stationed at the LA site, so splitting can occur on all healthy leukopak
requests and select disease state leukopaks
• As of now, only fresh half leukopaks are available (no quarters, tenths, etc)
HLA Typing:
• Sanguine is partnered with Labcorp to perform high resolution HLA typing on
leukopaks
• For healthy Leukopaks, HLA -A typing (the -A gene locus) is included for all
collections, but if clients want to select a specific -A allele, they must pay a fee
• Full Loci (HLA-A,B,C,D, DQ, etc..) is also available via Labcorp, but the is by request
only and is assessed a separate fee
• Disease state Leukopaks are NOT HLA-A typed, but can be on request (full loci also
available upon request)
Accellix FLOW:
• The San Diego Lab has an Accellix benchtop FLOW cytometer
• This machine can perform basic FLOW cytometry using pre-set panels
• As of now, only a T,B,NK enumeration and a T cell panel are available to clients
