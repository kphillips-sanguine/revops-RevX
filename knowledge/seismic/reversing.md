Reversing
Reversing is a tool where you answer a question with a question. Use this lesson to better understand how to
use it strategically.

Introduction
Reversing
The reversing technique involves responding to a question with another question,
promoting deeper self-reflection and insight.
By encouraging individuals to explore their own reasoning and emotions, reversing can
lead to a more profound understanding of issues and new perspectives.

---

<!-- Page 3 -->
Purpose of Reversing
One of the main purposes of using the reversing technique is to encourage the individual to
delve further into their own reasoning and emotions. By responding to a question with
another question, the responder prompts the person to explore their initial query from
different angles.
Reversing Purposes
• Gain clarity on the underlying question.
• Ensure you answer the right question.
• Gives you time to think about how you want to respond.
• Allows you to get more information before answering.
• Keeps you in control of the conversation.

Common Examples of Reversing
For example, if someone asks, "How long does it typically take to source samples?"
They could be asking for a variety of reasons (they have a specific end date they need all
the samples by, they are just genuinely curious and have no idea how long prospective
collections take etc).
How could you reverse this question?
"How long does it typically take to source samples?"
• It varies based on the complexity of the study. “Is there a specific end date that you
would need these samples by?”
• Study startup typically takes about a month, but there are ways to expedite it. “Is there a
specific end date that you would need these samples by?”
• It depends on your I/E criteria. Is that something you’d like to walk through now?
“How much does this typically cost?”
• It depends on the criteria (Number of samples, Disease State and I/E criteria) you’re
looking for. Can you tell me a little more about the samples you’re looking for?

Implementing Reversing
Implementing the reversing technique requires active listening and a genuine interest in
understanding the other person's perspective. When someone asks you a question, take a
pause and see if it makes sense to reverse.
You don’t need to answer every question with a question.

Enhancing Proficiency
In order to enhance one's proficiency in using the reversing technique, it is beneficial to
engage in training, workshops, or role-playing scenarios to practice this communication
skill. Additionally, engaging in real-life situations and receiving feedback can aid in refining
the ability to utilize reversing effectively.
When you are engaging in trainings, 1:1s, practice your reversing! It'll get you used to
answering questions with questions for your clients.

---

<!-- Page 8 -->
Reversing DO's
DO:
• Practice the common reversing scenarios so they sound natural.
• Have multiple ways to ask the same question.
• Make sure your tone is curious and sincere vs. dismissive and combative.

---

<!-- Page 9 -->
Reversing Don'ts
Don’ts:
• Reverse for the sake of reversing.
• Use the same softening statement repeatedly. For example, “That’s a great question”.
• Reverse after they’ve asked the same question twice.

more insight into the clients needs and why they are asking these questions. Through
practice and thoughtful application, the reversing technique can become a powerful skill in
fostering meaningful and insightful conversations.
