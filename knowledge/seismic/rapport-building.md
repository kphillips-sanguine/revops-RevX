What is Rapport
Rapport is relationship characterized by agreement, mutual understanding, or empathy that
makes communication possible or easy.
Rapport is established in seconds, relationships are built overtime.
The better you get at communicating, the more you will sell because PEOPLE BUY
FROM PEOPLE THEY LIKE.

Signs to Improve your Rapport Building
Here are some signs you may need to improve your rapport building skills
• Prospects seem disinterested or unresponsive during conversations
• Prospects are reluctant to share info - goals, challenges, budget
• Prospects mislead you about decision making process
• Prospects use your proposal to negotiate a better deal
• Prospects ghost you - disappear or become unresponsive
• Prospects don’t want to answer any of your questions

Build Genuine Rapport
How do you build GENUINE rapport?
• Connect on a personal level that is not contrived or forced (be interested in them!)
• Clear path to communication
• Have fun - you are selling to human beings!
• Ask the difficult questions that no one has asked
◦Showing you’re thinking about their business and pain points from a different
perspective
• Be aware of their DISC profile and communicate accordingly
• Communicate with your whole self (be aware of your body language!)

Small Talk
small talk leads to BIG talk
Chit-chat is an important place to practice effective communication. Ask interesting
questions and make connections, but no matter the situation, authenticity is key.
Small Talk Tips
• Use short sentences.
• Vary your speaking speed.
• Avoid fillers like "umm.." and "you know."
• Find a way to bridge the small talk to enter the conversation
◦“I could talk about this all day but I know we have only 30 minutes, should we dive
in?”
◦“Actually that’s exactly what I wanted to talk about today, so before we go into
that would it make sense to do some brief introductions, talk about XYZ and
ABC?”
• Start practicing this in your internal calls

Active Listening
• To effectively listen, clear your mind from distractions so you can focus your attention
on hearing and understanding what the speaker is saying.
• Don't jump to conclusions. Listen to everything the person has to say before
formulating and relaying your response.
• Listen emphatically
People want to be felt as well as heard. To make sure you really are getting the
message, repeat back what they said. This gives them an opportunity to clarify points
that may have been misunderstood.
Remember to ask questions (reverse) - you don't know the reason for why they are asking.
Tips
• Pay attention (do not multitask)
• Maintain eye contact without staring.
• Don't interrupt.
• Summarize what you heard.

Communication Theory
Mehrabian’s 7-38-55 Communication Model
Albert Mehrabian has concluded that only 7% of feelings and attitudes takes place
through the words we use in spoken communications, while 38% takes place
through tone and voice and the remaining 55% take place through body language.
It's easy to misinterpret non-verbal communication. If what you're seeing isn't matching
what you're hearing, ask for clarification.
