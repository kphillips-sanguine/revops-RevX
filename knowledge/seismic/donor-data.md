Donor Data Overview
Donor Data
Since Sanguine has direct-to-donor relationships, we do our best to get as much data
about the donor as possible to help enable the best science.
It gives a "full picture" of the donor which lends to greater analysis, and more specific
treatments being built in research. Let's break down all the types of data we provide.

Demographics
Demographics
Standard Participant Screening Data
• Demographics (Age, Sex, Ethnicity)
• Body measurement data (height and weight) - can calculate BMI if needed
• Primary diagnosis
• Comorbidities (additional diagnoses)
• Current medications
• Social habits (smoking, alcohol, tobacco, caffeine use)
• Proof of diagnosis
◦Laboratory results, medical records, physician note

SSQ
Study Specific Questions
Study Specific Questions (SSQs) are asked anytime there is specific Inclusion or
Exclusion criteria for the study. An example would be, "Are you on any of the following
medications: X, Y, X?"
As a reminder, the donors are BLINDED to these questions, meaning they don't know if
they answer yes or no to these questions if they will get them into the study or not. This
helps them to answer honestly.
We have standard questions we ask that follow our own protocol:
• Are you willing and able to provide appropriate photo identification to mobile
phlebotomist during at-home visit for verification of identity.
• Are you willing and able to provide appropriate written informed consent (as
applicable).
• Have you experienced excess blood loss, including blood donation defined as 250 mL
in the last month or 500 mL in the last two months.
• For requests that do not include subjects with infectious diseases, do you have
current, active HIV, hepatitis or other infectious diseases (self-reported or medical
history reviewed).
• Have you taken an investigational product in the last 30 days.
• For requests that do not include pregnant subjects, are you pregnant or nursing.

PRO
Patient Reported Outcomes (PRO)
PRO: Any report of the status of a patient's health condition that comes directly from the
donor, without interpretation of the patient's response by a clinician or anyone else. These
are often reported in the form of surveys, and are commonly used in clinical trials.
PROs can be a standalone service, or added to translational studies to provide more value.
Some PRO data points can include the following:
• Mental Health
• Fatigue
• Mobility
• Pain
• Productivity
• Opinions on Medication and Side Effects
• Flare Events
• Disease Localization

FHIR
FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources)
NOW LIVE
FHIR is a new platform we will leverage to get better insights into medical data. Adding
EMR extraction & data structuring capabilities bolsters and enhances the datasets that
Sanguine can provide researchers, enabling a holistic view of the patient & biospecimen to
make more accurate conclusions. This requires a one medical record signature.
FHIR allows us to extract and search structured data via patient authorized endpoints such
as:
• Conditions:
◦ICD10 (Classification system)
◦SNOMED (systematically organized computer-processable collection of medical
terms providing codes, terms, synonyms and definitions used in clinical
documentation and reporting)
• Medications (Prescription, Status, Dosage)
• Labs & Vital Signs (Lab name, lab value, issue date)
• Procedures (surgeries)
• Encounters (Doctor Appointments, hospital visits, reason for visit)
• Social History (Smoking status, alcohol use etc.)

Survey
If a survey is copyrighted, and we are going to use all of the questions from this survey with
the same scoring structure, we need to get permission because this particular survey is
under copywrite.
If the survey is open source, we don't require permission to use.

• Bristol Score (Stool)
• POEM Score (Atopic Derm)

SLEDAI
SLEDAI
Sanguine offers an adapted Sanguine questionnaire which functions similar to a flare study,
asking questions based around donor symptoms which lend to their severity of the disease.
• Medical Records: Historical SLEDAI scoring is unlikely to be available in Medical
Records (e.g. SLEDAI is not commonly performed in a clinical setting), but will depend
on condition/scoring method
• Self Report: Most donors do not know their historical scores. If this is the route the
client wants to go,
• Adapted disease scoring: In certain cases, Sanguine can obtain an “adapted” version
of the disease score requested, if the full version of the score is not possible (e.g.
requires lab results, physician assessment, etc.)
◦Option 1: Adapted SLEDAI (questionnaire only): subject-reported symptoms
factored in, but not lab results
◦Option 2: Adapted SLEDAI (with labs): subject-reported symptoms factored in,
along with lab results
▪Complement Testing
▪Urinalysis (Casts, Hematuria, Pyuria, Proteinuria)

EULAR/ESSAI
EULAR Sjögren's Syndrome Disease Activity Index (ESSDAI)
This test for Sjogren's Syndrome is a copyrighted survey that measures disease activity
including:
• Constitutional (Fevers, night sweats, weightloss)
• Lymphadenopathy
• Glandular Swelling
• Articular Involvement
• Cutaneous Involvement
• Pulmonary Involvement
Since this is copyrighted, we cannot ask these specific questions to determine a score, as
clinical assessment is also required. We can try to find this medical record value, but
similar to SLEDAI, it's not always available.

POEM Score
POEM Score
The Patient-Oriented Eczema Measure (POEM) is a self-assessed, repeatable
measurement tool for measuring atopic dermatitis severity. This is copyrighted and will
need to place a specific. request to get approval to administer this survey.
The POEM score asks donors questions about their Eczema within the last week, which
can be challenging to get an accurate depiction of their disease if they are not having a
flare during that time.
We recommend trying to base on self-reported severity vs doing the POEM scoring.
