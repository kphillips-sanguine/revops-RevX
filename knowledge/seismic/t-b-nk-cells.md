Overview
What are T, B, and NK Cells
• T, B, and NK cells are types of lymphocytes (type of white blood cell)
T, B, NK Cell Overview
• T and B cells are part of the adaptive immune system → learned and remembers
• NK cells are part of the innate immune system → immediate and general protection
• T, B, and NK cells are all components of PBMC
T Cells:
• T cells - attack and destroy infected and cancerous cells
◦Cytotoxic (CD8+) - attack and kill (infections and cancer)
◦Helper (CD4+) - coordinate by signaling other immune cells
◦Regulatory (Tregs) - suppress immune responses (cancer and autoimmune)
◦Memory - remember past infections
• CD3+ or Pan T cells - includes helper, cytotoxic, regulatory and NK cells

---

<!-- Page 3 -->
B Cells:
• B cells - produce infection fighting antibodies
• CD19+ - recognize and produce antibodies to neutralize pathogens and toxins
NK Cells:
• NK cells - rapid nonspecific response
• CD56+ - Eliminate cells infected with virus, cancerous, and other abnormal cells
