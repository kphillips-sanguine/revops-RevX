Introduction to Sanguine
Sanguine Mission Statement:
Sanguine Biosciences is accelerating personalized medicine by empowering
patients with their healthcare data and facilitating participation in biomedical
research. Sanguine actively bridges the gap between patients and researchers
through direct-to-patient engagement, in-home collection of fully annotated
biospecimens, apheresis product offerings, and a suite of biorepository
services.
Sanguine has supported translational, clinical, and cell & gene therapy research at
35 of the top 40 global pharmaceutical and biotechnology companies, shortening
timelines and validating early-stage results to increase confidence and refine
biomarkers in human trials."

Sanguine mission, highlights the WHY behind Sanguine
One of the founders, Brian Neman, was inspired by a high school friend who was
diagnosed with multiple sclerosis (MS) at the young age of 12. In high school she was a
strong patient advocate from donating her own blood for research to starting a patient
advocacy group called Youth Against MS. This group grew to 5,000 members across the
US and demonstrated to Neman “the power of engagement and advocacy for the purposes
of advancing research.” Neman along with co-founder Gerald Lee set out to lower the
barrier for people to participate in research for conditions that ail them by donating much
needed biospecimens, and thus created the direct-to-donor model. Neman describes how
“if we decrease the burden, we will have a community of people wanting to participate, and
we make it easy for them to do just that from obtaining their electronic health records to
coming to their home for sample collection.” Ultimately, connecting the researchers with
donor biospecimens is a necessary piece to accelerating discovery.

History
Sanguine started in 2010 with our co-founders, Brian Neman and Gerald Lee in Los
Angelos. When Sanguine first started, we did develop maintain a biobank, but we primarily
had a prospective model with our mobile phlebotomist and began to steadily build and
grow our current model. When Covid hit in 2020, we were already set up for success with
our remote model and we were able to assist with phlebotomy for clinical trials and identify
covid samples for research, which helped catapult our growth.
We moved our official headquarters to Massachusetts in fall of 2021.
In the summer of 2022, we opened our own lab in San Diego to bring more of our sample
processing in house. This allowed us more control over the sample processing to allow
custom processing, clinical pbmc processing services, and more availability to process
samples and complete studies faster.
In the winter, we started getting into the Leukopak market, delivering our first diseased
leukopak in December 2022.
In September 2023, we opened our own Apheresis Center in Los Angeles, where we now
collect healthy and disease state leukopaks.
In 2024, we launched Electronic Health Record software to help provide more patient data,
speed up recruitment and enrollment timelines.
Sanguine has plans to add GMP and Mobilized Leukopaks in 2024/2025.
Our leadership is dedicated to Sanguine's success and continues to invest in our
organization to add more products and accelerate our own growth, as we accelerate
research for many organizations and patient populations.
