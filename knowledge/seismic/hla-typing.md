What is HLA Typing?
Human leukocyte antigen (HLA) typing is essential in understanding the genetic variations
of the human leukocyte antigen system, which plays a crucial role in immune responses.
HLA typing is the process of sequencing a donor's various genes that make up their HLA
loci.
HLA typing can be performed in low-resolution (antibody based), medium resolution (NGS
based), or high-resolution (NGS based)
• Sanguine employs high resolution, 4 field HLA typing
Alleles & Loci
HLA Loci are separated into Class I and Class II segments. HLA Class I is often the most
important to clients, and within class I, specifically, Loci "-A"
These do not need to memorized, but can be referenced in this lesson for future questions
by clients
Class I:
• HLA-A
• HLA-B
• HLA-C
Class II:

Why HLA Typing
HLA is used to match patients and donors for bone marrow, cord blood, or cell therapy
transplants. HLA are proteins -- or markers -- found on most cells in your body. Your
immune system uses these markers to recognize which cells belong in your body and
which do not.
By identifying specific HLA alleles associated with autoimmune disorders, allergies,
infectious diseases, and certain cancers, researchers can gain insights into disease
mechanisms and potential therapeutic targets. Moreover, HLA typing facilitates the
development of personalized medicine by aiding in the prediction of individual immune
responses and treatment outcomes based on HLA profiles.
While HLA typing is relevant for all sanguine specimen products, we see the most interest
for leukopak related products.
Due to HLA's importance in cell therapy donor matching, clients often must know the
donor's HLA type prior to receiving their leukopaks.
This is particularly important for healthy leukopaks, and clients often expect this
information to have already been typed.
The HLA-A locus is the most important locus for cell therapy research, and we have found
that ~90% of our requests for specific donor alleles are for HLA-A (e.g. HLA-02:01)
Because of this, Sanguine has made the strategic decision to HLA-A type all healthy
donors from the LA site. All of this information is uploaded into Salesforce, and our
operations/recruitment team can search for donors by HLA-A alleles and consent them into
new leukopak collection projects

HLA Vendor & Tests
As of 2024, LabCorp performs all HLA typing for Sanguine products. The offer two different
options at this time
HLA-A typing
• This testing option only types the "A" locus.
• Sanguine is performing this service free of charge for all healthy apheresis donors, but
if clients want to select a specific allele from an already typed donor, there is a fee
($300).
• For translational studies or other sample types where Sanguine is not performing HLA-
A typing as a standard, the client has the option to add this test ad-hoc for a fee of
$150
Full HLA Typing
• This testing option types all loci (all Class I & Class II loci)
• This is only performed by client request, for a fee of $650

Sample Types
There are two possible sample types that Labcorp accepts for HLA typing:
• Buccal Swabs
• Whole Blood (EDTA)
For leukapheresis, we are always using whole blood as the sample type. For translational
studies, either approach can be used.
Pricing is uniform for both options, and labcorp has an expected turnaround time (TAT) of
10 days post sample receipt

Screening Visits
Since Sanguine, for the most part, only has HLA information on file for HLA-A, and only for
healthy apheresis donors, if clients are asking for donors with a specific allele (e.g. HLA-
DRB1:02), then they will have to pay to screen a subset of donors until this allele is found.
From there, allele confirmed specific donors can be recalled for any sanguine sample type
Pricing is dependent upon the opportunity record type of interest.
