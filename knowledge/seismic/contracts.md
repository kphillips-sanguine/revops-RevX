Contracts
Contracts are a very important topic in moving a deal forward. Sometimes contracting can
take a couple days, other times it can take months or longer. It is very important to know
what we need to have in place to protect our company and how to ask what the client
needs so we don't have any hiccups along the way.
This lesson walks through the most common contracts we see and how to handle them. All
of our contracts are currently saved in the Revenue Drive for you to reference and look at
prior to a client call. To get a contract signed, we use sales signatures request on Slack.
The content below is a good reference for contracting guidelines and process as they
change.

Confidentiality Agreements
CDA
Confidential Disclosure Agreement
NDA
Non-Disclosure Agreement
CDAs and NDAs are used interchangeably depending on the organization and both allow
for confidentiality between both parties.
For both CDA and NDA, we can use one of our templates (preferred), as leveraging other
parties can delay reviews. We want to have MUTUAL NDAs and CDAs ensuring all parties
agree not to reveal each other's confidential information.
NDAs & CDAs should be fully executed (signed by all parties) prior to the sharing of
any Sanguine related information, including a copy of our MSA and any proposals.

MSA / SOWs
MSA
Master Services Agreement
An MSA will ensure that there is consistent work provided between two parties for current
and future work. It establishes terms and conditions between two companies so that
future agreements are fast-tracked. This agreement provides protections to both parties.
Some topics that are covered in MSAs are the following:
1. Expiration terms
2. Party associated with agreements (subsidiaries, affiliated companies)
3. Biomaterial Use & Restrictions
4. Payment terms (we prefer Net 30!)
5. Cancellation terms
6. Insurance requirements
7. Acceptance of Biomaterial
8. Scope of services
SOW
Statement of Work; The SOW is an appendix in the MSA which denotes the format all

Scientist.com
We have our own MSA contracted with Scientist.com and ScienceExchange. You do not
need individual contracting with those companies to do business on those platforms.
When you submit a proposal on their platform, oftentimes clients call them Statements of
Work. You do not need a special SOW for these opportunities, you just have to input it into
the platform and attach our formal proposal for reference.

MTA
MTA
Materials Transfer Agreement
MTAs are very similar to MSAs, however one difference is MTAs do NOT require formal
SOWs for proposals. This makes the contracting process a little easier because the
scientists/clients just need to acknowledge they want to move forward (documented via
email, or signing the standard proposal).
When to use an MTA....
• If the client requests an MSA and their study doesn't meet our MSA requirements, it's
always good to suggest MTA to make contracting easier and shorten the Sales
Signature Process
• Aligns better with the following products:
◦Disease Leukos
◦Healthy Leukos
◦Prospective Collections - Tier 1-4 no lab component

POs
PO
A purchase order (PO) is a formal offer from a buyer to a seller to purchase products or
services at a specific price
A PO is required for all Closed/Won opportunities. The PO details how we BILL the client
so it needs to match the total amount of the opportunity. It is also important that the
upfront is noted on the PO so we can bill appropriately.
PO only is acceptable for....
• Inventory
• Healthy Leukos
• Leuko Lots

Vendor Qualification
It is very important to ask NEW clients if there is anything we need to have in place in order
for them to work with us. This can range from contracts (CDA, MTA, MSA) and/or vendor
qualification forms.
Some clients ask us to fill out a "Vendor Questionnaire," some clients say they just need
our W9 (located in Seismic), others may request a formal audit. Our quality team will fill out
the vendor questionnaire and perform audits. These can be very time intensive so it's good
to know when they need their samples and how quick we can move this process. Loop in
the quality team at any mention of an audit!
We only share our formal SOPs during an audit.
