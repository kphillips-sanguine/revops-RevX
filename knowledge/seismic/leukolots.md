Background
In 2023, Sanguine launched its first apheresis clinic in Los Angeles, CA to support the
collection of apheresis products, mainly leukopaks, from its 70,000+ engaged patient
community.
Leukapheresis collections yield products that are enriched with white blood cells/
PBMCs at a much higher concentration than typical whole blood collections, and
average leukapheresis collections yield > 10 Billion+ Total Nucleated Cells (TNCs)
from a single donation.
Since the opening of the Los Angeles apheresis site, Sanguine has sought to continue to
increase site utilization by scheduling leukopak collections throughout the site's operating
hours.
In a normal leukopak collection workflow:
1. each scheduled leukopak collection, disease state or healthy, is assigned to an open
client request based on their specific I/E criteria requirements
2. these leukopaks are either shipped directly to the client fresh, or they are sent to the
Sanguine lab in San Diego, CA for further processing, cell isolation, and
cryopreservation.
3. However, there are times when apheresis donors are available and ready to donate
during a collection slot, and there is no immediate end buyer for the collected
leukopak.
4. Faced with this scenario, Sanguine has decided to start building out its own leukopak-

Product Description
Sanguine LeukoLots™ are cryopreserved “lots” of PBMCs that have been isolated from
leukapheresis collections as inventory at the San Diego, CA biobank. Sanguine has decided
to create LeukoLots from any unallocated Leukopak collections at the Los Angeles site,
and occasionally, half LeukoLots are created from half collections in which case the other
half Leukopak is sent to an immediate end user/customer.
Sanguine LeukoLots vary in size since donor-to-donor variation can result in a wide
range of final leukopak cell yields, and differences in the average yield between
healthy and different disease states.

Healthy LeukoLots
Healthy LeukoLot Composition
Each Sanguine Healthy LeukoLot contains exactly 40 x 100M cell aliquots of PBMCs,
for a guaranteed total cell count of 4 billion PBMCs per full LeukoLot
Each Sanguine Healthy LeukoLot also contains ~12 x 25M cell aliquots of PBMCs
that are available as “screening vials" from the same donor.
PBMC isolation is performed via Sanguine's standard PBMC isolation protocol using a
density gradient medium
Cryopreservation is done using CryoStor 10 as the freezing medium
LeukoLots are stored in LN2 dewars
I/E Criteria
Specific I/E requirements for banked lots should be limited, and will only be considered on
a case-by-case basis (e.g. age, demographic criteria asks for lots to be screened)
All of these donors will be HLA-A typed.

Customer Value & Differentiators
The value of Sanguine LeukoLots for Sanguine customers is similar to
leukopaks in the following ways:
• High Volume of cells from a single donor, allowing for a source of control material that
yields reproducibility across multiple experiments
• Assay validation
• Cost-effectiveness vs similar cell yields isolated from whole-blood
• Isolation of rare cell subsets
The additional value of Sanguine LeukoLots compared to leukopaks is
present in the following ways:
• No Further isolation needed– LeukoLots are pre-isolated by Sanguine's lab staff
• Longer shelf life than fresh leukopaks
• Higher average recovery and viability than cryopreserved full leukopaks
• Pre-aliquoted vials allow for splitting amongst multiple R&D groups within an
organization
The Key differentiator that Sanguine's customers recognize by using LeukoLots is
that the ability to screen vials from individual LeukoLots and reserve these lots for up
to 30 days lowers the barrier of entry for ideal donor identification.
Often, Sanguine customers need a chance to screen a small quantity of PBMCs from
multiple donors before committing to a large volume leukopak or LeukoLot purchase.

---

<!-- Page 20 -->
Since LeukoLots are isolated in individual vials, Sanguine can send a vial from multiple
donors to an end user, the end user screens these cells and then can identify which
donor's cells work best for their specific research goals.
Once these ideal donors are identified, the customer can select which LeukoLots they
would like to purchase to obtain a high quantity of cells from the selected donor(s).
Customers may be trying to identify the following when screening LeukoLots:
• Specific Biomarker signatures/levels
• High quantities of a desired immune cell subtype for a given donor (e.g. a donor with
abnormally high CD19+ B cells))
• Uncommon HLA Alleles
• Responders to a researcher's specific assay
What are some similarities between Sanguine LeukoLots and leukopaks?
High volume of cells from a single donor, Assay validation, Cost-effectiveness, Isolation of
rare cell subsets

Conclusion
Sanguine LeukoLots™ offer a valuable resource for researchers seeking specific cell types
for their experiments, with high volumes of cells from a single donor. The ability to screen
and reserve lots for up to 30 days lowers the barrier of entry for ideal donor identification,
providing customers with the opportunity to identify the most suitable cells for their
research goals. This unique approach, coupled with the pre-isolation and longer shelf life,
sets Sanguine LeukoLots™ apart from traditional leukopaks.
