Community Access Overview
Community Access is one of Sanguine's services that focuses on streamlining fulfillment of
multiple translational biospecimen studies in specified patient populations to cut costs and
accelerate results.
Community Access acts as a blanket contract for Patient Access to a specific indication(s)
to:
• Maximize Flexibility
• Minimize Contracting
• Save time and startup fees for launching new studies
There are two Opportunity Record Types for Community Access:
1. Community Access Parent - this serves as the original contract
2. Community Access Related - this monitors the individual requests under the
original contract
The Community Access Parent contract renews on an annual basis.
This is primarily focused on Translational Studies.

Use Cases
When does it make sense to pitch Community Access:
• Client is doing several studies for the same indication(s)
• Client needs flexibility in study design within the same indications
◦Changes in tube types, blood volumes, sample types, criteria
▪Proven by multiple change orders in previous studies, or expressed interest to
make future changes in the study
• Client has expressed interest in the following study designs:
◦Screening: Trying to find a very specific donor, this will give them access to
screen as many patients as they need with a capped patient access fee
◦Longitudinal Studies: Large longitudinal studies typically have a % of drop out.
This can allow us to have flexibility to add in additional patients to account for that
▪Potentially add more recalls
• Client needs faster turnaround timelines
• Client needs to minimize contracting (“Blanket Contract”)
Benefits to Client
• Cost savings (only one startup, no change order fees)
◦Potential for PID savings depending on utilization

Questions to ask
• What indications are they interested in?
◦Sometimes clients have 1, sometimes they have a group of AutoImmune Diseases
they want to be able to pick from
• How many samples do they anticipate needing over the course of the year?
• How many studies do they anticipate having?
• Will there be multiple groups ordering? Or just one?
◦We can do separate POs for the biospecimen collections to allow multiple groups
to order within their own budget
All of these can help build out your value proposition, and will help decide how we price
this out.

Pricing
Pricing
• Upfront:
◦Study Startup Fee (standard, one-time)
◦Patient Access:
▪1. One time upfront payment for unlimited access
▪2. One time upfront payment for specified number
▪If they go over this, they can pull from collection funds (if available for
more) OR do a change order OR renew contract early with more funds
▪We really want to push unlimited access because it adds more value from
the client side, but depending on the number needed, we will evaluate it.
▪3. $ per Patient, Charged upfront at launch of each study
▪This is more of the blanket contract style - may make sense for smaller
companies and to reduce risk for them
• Biospecimen Collection and Premium Services Funds
◦“Draw down fund” - used as studies occur, billed off a rate card

Objection Handling
• What happens if we pay upfront and then you can’t find the patients we need?
◦Share fulfillment rate for that indication or therapeutic area! Huge selling point
◦Typically we only do community access for communities in which we have an
engaged and a higher number of patients, so that way we are able to handle
various requests and I/E
◦*Client still pushes back* → We can build in rollover language to swap our
communities or rollover a certain amount of patients to another contract if we can’t
accommodate the majority of your requests. For example:
▪Additional communities will be subject to an additional patient recruitment fee
which will vary depending on condition.
▪Client may swap communities of similar value (based on disease prevalence
and our community size) upon request and Sanguine’s discretion
• Why does this renew on an annual basis?
◦These contracts are built on providing the patient access for a year; The renewal
allows us to reassess: The communities you need, number of patients, and pricing
changes
▪We also add more patients each year to each community
• What happens if we don’t use up all of the samples/don't use our estimated
number of samples this year? Can they rollover?
◦We try and estimate the number of samples you will use over the course of the
