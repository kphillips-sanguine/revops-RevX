Plasma
Plasma
Whole Blood can be centrifuged and aliquoted to create plasma.
• Spinning Process:
◦Spin for 10 minutes, 2500rpm, Transfer by pipette
◦Yields 50% of the blood volume (100mL = 50mL plasma)
◦Can “Double Spin” for Platelet Rich Plasma (PRP - 20% yield from whole blood),
Platelet Poor Plasma (PPP - 30% yield from whole blood) if requested
Usage:
• General health of the patient (immune health through nutrients and proteins)
• Specific disease treatment
• Biomarker Identification
Standard Tubes / Anticoagulants and usage:
• Sodium Hep (Recommended for PBMC)

Serum
Serum
Whole Blood can be centrifuged and aliquoted to create serum.

Usage:

PBMCs
Peripheral blood mononuclear cells (PBMCs) are immune cells characterized by the
presence of a single round nucleus. PBMCs consist of lymphocytes (T cells, B cells, NK
cells), monocytes, and dendritic cells. PBMCs derived from human blood are commonly
used in preclinical and clinical research.
Sanguine will collect whole blood, ship overnight to the Sanguine lab and process into
PBMCs.
Sanguine typically assumes a 1:1 ratio. 1mL of whole blood = 1M (million) PBMCs. This
does vary depending on:
• Disease / Condition
• Processing and thawing
We do have our own processing protocol but can evaluate the clients as well. We can
aliquot into 5M or 10M cell vials. 10M is our standard. When a client is looking for a lot of
PBMCs, this is when you may want to pivot to Leukopaks.

Urine
Urine
Types of Urine Collections:
• First Void: First urine of the day
◦If they want first void, we would send a collection cup in advance
• Second Void: Second urine of the day
• Anytime Void: Urine any time of day
• Mid-Stream collections: Urinate in toilet, then collect mid-steam in cup
• 24 hour collections (into a jug)
• Centrifugation
Study Designs:
• We can send urine cups in advance.
◦Any kidney diseases or diseases that impact the ability to urinate should be sent in
advance
• Donors are able to put their urine in their fridge to keep stable until phlebotomist
arrives
• We can send them to the client refrigerated or on dry ice

Stool
Stool
• Stool collection kits are sent in advance
• Standard stool collection procedure: subject self-collects within 24 hours of home visit,
stores in home freezer until visit, and phlebotomist ships on dry ice
◦Stool samples may also be refrigerated prior to phlebotomist arrival, if preferred, or
if additives are required
• If stool samples require aliquoting, this must be done by the subjects themselves prior
to storing in home freezer
• Sanguine cannot guarantee the amount of stool a patient produces
• Additives we have done within the home:
◦PBS and Glycerol solution
◦AnaeroPouch
▪Anaerobic Conditions: This means the oxygen is removed from the sample

Sputum
Sputum
Sputum is the mucous or phlegm coughed up from your lungs. It is not saliva or mucous
from the back of the throat. Typically, about a tablespoon of sputum is the amount needed.
We cannot do INDUCED sputum.
We can do a saline rinse and gargle protocol to help increase the amount of sputum a
donor produces
Relevant Opportunities:
• COPD Sputum Study
Sputum Value
• The analysis of sputum is essential for discovering the reasons leading to different airway
and lung diseases (ex. COPD)

Skin Taping
Skin Taping
Skin Taping: Used for dermatology, drug development, skin microbiome and skin biology
• Standard Skin Taping Procedure: Cuderm Strips & storage cards (without pressure
device)
• Only takes the top layer of skin (top levels of epidermis not dermis), no punctures/
fibroblasts allowed within our protocol
• Max of 50 strips within the home
*Always ask if this process is okay.
Note: We can do Dermtech (must be ordered by client) and we can do pressure device if
requested
Shipping temperatures
• Skin tapes in cryovials typically require shipping frozen
• Skin tapes on storage cards can be shipped ambient
Collection areas:
• Volar forearm unless otherwise specified

Challenges
Challenges
Some challenges come up across all sample types:
Blood
• We cannot go over the volumes designated by the disease within the 6 week period. If
the client wants 100mL but the patient is a hard stick in the first visit and gives 20mL,
we will have to wait 6 weeks to try again or find a new patient
• Serum/Plasma within the home - we are still limited by the volume due to the aliquot
limits and fees
◦If a client wants a high volume, pivot to the lab or request larger aliquot sizes
Sputum
• We cannot do induced. This can be challenging to get patients to produce sputum.
Stool
• Phlebotomists will not aliquot stool. The patient will have to do it.
Skin Taping
• We cannot do skin punctures or fibroblasts. These are considered invasive.
