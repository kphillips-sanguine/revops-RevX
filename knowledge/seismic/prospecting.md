Introduction
Prospecting in sales is crucial for identifying potential customers and driving successful
sales. Understanding your target audience's needs and utilizing the right tools and
techniques are key to effective prospecting.
Implementing best practices and setting clear objectives can optimize your efforts and lead
to higher success rates for booking meetings, meeting conversions and building pipeline.

Objective Setting
Effective prospecting requires a strategic and well-thought-out plan to ensure that you are
targeting the right audience and increasing the probability of converting leads into
champions.
Setting clear objectives for prospecting is imperative to keep your efforts focused and
measurable. For example, your quota is focused on number of meetings and number of
opps generated from those meetings. To meet your quotas, you need to set objectives for
how to get there. For example:
• How many people do I need to reach out to generate one meeting?
• How many LQCs do I typically take before I can convert to an opportunity?
For Account Executives, your quotas are based on bookings. You need your pipeline to be
2x your goal at a minimum. Look at your average opportunity size. How many opportunities
do you need to meet that quota? What is your conversion rate from meeting to
opportunity? How many people do you need to reach out to to score those meetings?
By setting achievable goals, sales professionals can gauge the effectiveness of their
prospecting strategies and make necessary adjustments to maximize results. Monitor your
own metrics and make tweaks to get there.
Additionally, having a clear understanding of the sales funnel and the stages in the
prospecting process can guide the approach taken with each potential lead, ultimately
leading to more successful conversions and long-term customer relationships.

Understanding the target audience
To prospect effectively, it is crucial to understand the needs and pain points of your target
audience. This involves conducting thorough market research and using the right tools and
techniques to gather relevant information. Utilizing social media platforms, industry
networking events, and our internal CRM can provide valuable insights into potential leads
and networking opportunities. By understanding your target market, you can tailor your
prospecting efforts and effectively communicate the value of your offerings.
You should follow the Personas for each product set in the product training to know what
messaging to use based on each persona and account type. It's important to recognize
that a CSO at a small startup company and a CSO at a top 10 pharma company are going
to be very different personas. For example, a CSO at a small biotech will likely be involved
with a deal and a great person to target. However, a CSO at Pfizer will likely not respond to
your email.
How you target a CSO at a small biotech vs a Scientist at that same biotech should also be
very different messages. For example, the Scientist is likely the end user so there are
certain things that will appeal to them - disease states, number of cells from a leukopak
etc. Whereas the CSO is focused on higher-level items - optimizing their study designs to
match their clinical trials, less duplication of lab efforts = cost savings, verified patients to
provide confidence in their results.
For these higher-up personas - look at their LinkedIn, look at the company message/values
and tie those into your messaging to show you're researching and we align.

Implementing best practices
Implementing best practices for prospecting is essential in optimizing your efforts and
ensuring a higher success rate. Building and maintaining a robust online presence through
social media, content marketing, and email campaigns can also drive awareness and
engage potential leads. Through our software with Salesforce and Gong Engage, you
should be able to identify what are the best accounts and leads and prioritize them
accordingly:
• Learn about our history with that account
◦Do we have a CDA/MSA in place?
◦Have they worked with us in the past?
◦Have we had other opportunities with them?
◦What is the account type?
• Pipeline alignment (see qualification training!)
• Identify cold contacts - leverage these relationships and familiarity with Sanguine
◦When did they use us last?
◦Are they still at the company?
◦Are they at a new company where I can leverage that previous relationship?
• Identify who is currently working with us
◦AEs should be reaching out to these folks and trying to generate referrals
• Send customized email cadences

Selling Value
Our market is becoming more commoditized so we need to adjust our language to make
sure we are standing out and highlighting our differentiating factors:
• Electronic Health Records
• Recallability of our patients
• Rare Diseases
• Customizable study designs
• Pipeline alignment within our community
Just saying we have samples isn't enough any more. We need to stand out and give a
reason for this person to want to respond to us.
Conciseness
Sending a long essay of an email isn't going to catch someone's attention as they are
flipping through their emails in between meetings. You need something short and sweet to
catch their attention and that proves value out the gate. I always recommend reading your
email out loud - does it flow? how does it sound? is it information overload where you
could split it up to two different emails?
Consider using the shorten tool in Gong engage. Think about the email subject lines - is
this something that would catch your attention? How are you standing out? I recommend
trying out a few for testing and see.

Customization
The more saturated our market is becoming, the more customized our messaging needs to
be. As mentioned earlier, we need to understand the persona we are reaching out to, the
account and their needs.
You should be following the news for updates on accounts including Fierce Biotech and
Endpoints to monitor for:
• Funding
• Mergers & Acquisitions
• Changes into new indications
• Clinical Trial updates
• Layoffs (a sign to slow down prospecting on those accounts)
◦Make sure you're connected with anyone we've had conversations with or done
business with in case they move to another account
Use these items in the messaging to show you are paying attention to their company and
how we can add value. For example:
Hi Name,
I saw ABC is moving into Lupus research. As many companies focused on CAR-T
are starting to move into the autoimmune space, there is a greater need for
leukopaks from these indications. Sanguine has over 2000+ Lupus patients in our
community, and we can get ~10 Billion cells per leukopak. Is this something you
may need?"

TIP
If you are reaching out to a small biotech with <20 employees, the chances these employees
are sitting next to each other is highly likely. If you send the same message to every single one
of those employees, they will likely discard your message and not respond.
You can start with a few, and then pause. Do not send it to all of them at the same time.
Tweak the message based on the persona.

Cadences
Cold Call Cadences
It takes about 6 touches for a lead to answer. Some times we think we are reaching out too
much, but often, it's not enough.
You should have a variety of cadences and should be monitoring the performance of those
cadences - watch the open rates, the click rates, the reply rates. If you have a cadence
with a low open/reply rate, it is probably time to update, do some A/B testing and
CUSTOMIZE!
There should be a variety of cold-call cadences based on:
• Account Type
• Account
• Persona
• Product
• Indications
You should have more than one cadence. For example, it can be beneficial to have
someone go through a cadence, then take a 2 month long break and get nurtured from
marketing, then add to a different cadence.
Meeting Follow Up Cadences
A meeting follow up cadence helps keep you aligned with next steps:

Nurture Cadences / Qualified No Opp

All of these emails should be customized based on your conversation with them. It's
important to reflect back on the emails, meetings etc to review.
They should also be spaced out longer - once a month or so. This is a great time to send
updates with our product set, new marketing materials, new case studies that are relevant
etc.

Prospecting Mistakes
MISTAKES
1. Small Accounts - sending them all the exact same message.
a. Choose key people to break into the account and send customized messages.
Space them out.
2. Pay attention to activity - don't waste time personalizing something to someone on
LinkedIn who doesn't have a lot of activity
3. Just accepting the "No"
a. Try and push back and ask more relevant questions to gain broader insight. For
example:
i. "Not relevant" - ask for a referral
ii. Not interested - "is this not a need right now, or do you not use patient
samples?"
iii. No need right now - ask when would be a good time to follow up, what type of
samples they typically use, push for an LQC for future needs....
iv. "aware of what you guys do - no need right now" - we've had a lot of updates
to our products, push for a call to see if any of our new services would be
relevant; if still no, ask for a referral
4. Over customizing: Customization is great, but you shouldn't be wasting an hour on one
email because then your productivity will go down substantially. You should have a
balance.
