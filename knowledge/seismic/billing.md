Billing Overview
We often get questions on how our billing works. It's important that we are able to explain
the different billing options to clients.

---

<!-- Page 3 -->
Upfront
Upfront Fees are fees we charge at the beginning of a study. Once we receive the PO, we
can bill for startup activities for the entire upfront. This is the fastest way for us to recognize
revenue.
Oftentimes the following line items go into Upfront Billing:
• Study Startup Fees
• IRB Fees
• Kitting
• Subject Reimbursement
• Patient Access & Reservation

---

<!-- Page 4 -->
Per Visit
When line items are billed PER VISIT, they are billed after visits occur. Typically we would
bill at the end of the month but we are trying to bill more frequently, unless noted in the
MSA.
When presenting a proposal, we would say these are billed after the visits occur. On the
translational side, some of the line items would be:
• Project Management
• CLIA Services
• Pre-Analytical Services
• Home Visit Services
• Shipping & Logistics

---

<!-- Page 5 -->
Manual Billing
There are some items that are billed MANUALLY, which means we only bill them IF they
occur. This usually applies to batch shipments or some data services.
If a client wants to put some extra batch shipments onto their proposal in case they do
want to ship prior to the end of a study, we will only bill for the batch shipments and
manually services that occur.

---

<!-- Page 6 -->
Monthly Billing
For onsite programs, we bill monthly for certain fees such as:
• Project Management
• Phleb Management
• Data Management
For storage only deals, we bill monthly for storage fees to recognize revenue as the time
goes on.

---

<!-- Page 7 -->
Net Terms
Our standard payment terms are Net 30. This means the client has 30 days to pay us
AFTER we invoice them. If we have an MSA with the client, they may negotiate the net
terms to try and extend them.
Anything on Scientist.com is Net 90.
