Indirect model has to go through a third party to find and access donor - either through
clinical sites, physician relationships, academic research etc.
Sanguine's Direct-to-Donor model is as simple as that. We connect directly with donors
within our community vs having to go through a third party such as a clinical site,
physicians office etc.
Our model gives us the ability to offer researchers a variety of services and additional value
including:
• Greater access to donors
• Donor Data
• Biospecimens

Indirect Challenges: Biobank
Biobank Challenges
• Poor quality of specimen (viability can be inconsistent)
• Can’t choose I/E or collection criteria
• They come with limited data sets
• Aren’t always collected in the correct method they need (tubes, immediate
centrifugation..)
• Not enough volume
• Likely cannot recall from these donors

---

<!-- Page 8 -->
Indirect Challenges: Prospective
Indirect Prospective Challenges
• Donor Access through physicians/clinics can be a really slow process because it takes
a lot of time to get those physician relationships set up and then your timeline is
dependent on donors going to see the doctor, agreeing to enroll in the study etc
◦Can emphasize this more with high risk donors (not always going to the doctor due
to sickness exposure concerns)
◦Trouble for longitudinal studies (if they cancel their appointment or don’t show up)
◦This also limits the control over choosing the donors because you are bound by a
specific geographical area
• Often times we have heard from our clients using these methods: that they've been
given timelines for 6 months but 2 years later they don’t have collections completed)
◦Try and see if our clients can relate to any of these challenges

• Oftentimes what we are seeing in this space is that researchers are obtaining patient
samples and data through a variety of models – including biobanks, CROs, data
resellers, which is what we refer to as an “Indirect Donor Model”

◦Lack of Access to Donors
◦Lack of Clinically Relevant Biospecimens and Data

• Having to stray from your ideal study design
• Sample variability
• Inconclusive data.
