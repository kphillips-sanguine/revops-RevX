Introduction
In this lesson, we will explore the concept of employer on-site biospecimen collections.
This innovative approach allows organizations to engage employees in contributing to
scientific research efforts.
By establishing on-site collection programs, companies can access convenient and
expedient biospecimens from their workforce, supporting a wide range of research
purposes. Let's delve into the benefits and implications of this unique method for
advancing scientific goals within the workplace.

Evaluating onsite requests
Onsite Programs are great programs, that are renewable revenue for Sanguine. We have a
95% fulfillment rate with onsite programs and most clients renew year over year.
However, there are a few factors that determine if an Onsite Program will be a fit for a
client. Review the entire Onsite Feasibility guide here.
• How many samples do they need per year?
◦~100 is the recommended minimum amount (We have done smaller though)
◦Another option is a same-day program
• How many employees do they have in that location?
◦This shows us how big the pool will be for draws
◦We usually see around 6-10% of employees sign up (based on large pharma)
◦60-70% retention for future draws
• Are they limiting this program to certain groups, or expanding to the entire location/
organization?
◦This helps us understand the recruitment needs and program limitations
• Can they commit to certain days?
◦Clients who only need adhoc coverage - it's hard to find a phlebotomist and retain
them with an inconsistent schedule

Finding Value in Onsite Programs
We have clients come to us to launch these programs for a variety of reasons:
• FRESH samples. Whether they be used for single cell assays, immunological assays,
the ability to get them within minutes of collection allows researchers to enable the
best science for their experiments.
• Control samples for other projects
• Quick turnaround for urgent/ inconsistent needs: Some researchers don’t know weeks
in advance when they will need samples, so being able to have a quick turnaround time
and flexibility is very helpful to perform the experiments they need, when they need
and how they need them.
• Fast turn around from the request to delivery (sample cannot be shaken during transit,
sensitive cells)
• On the Employer Side:
◦It’s a great way to engage employees to participate in R&D for the organization
◦Which can also result in Savings for the organization
▪Reliability and knowledge of handling reduce variability = may need fewer
samples to get high quality data
▪Getting samples Fast = saves time

Quality
Clients handling Onsite Programs internally face the following
challenges:
• Drain on internal resources
• Creating and abiding by privacy policies, specifically the blinding and anonymization of
participants
• Must create SOPs for all processes, and develop a privacy policy.
• HIPAA risk: By utilizing internal resources for either phlebotomy or screening,
consenting and scheduling, there is a higher risk of non-compliance because these
employees are unblinded to the participants and their samples.
◦To ensure extra privacy, once the donation is complete and the donor has left the
area, the phlebotomist will notify the researcher the blood is ready and provide the
pickup location for them to instantly get their blood, ensuring the privacy that
donor and researcher are not passing in the halls
CUSTOM PROTOCOL
Sanguine mandates all Onsite Programs have a custom protocol. This allows the client
flexibility to make the protocol their own, but it also adds a startup cost of ~12K and a 6-8
week startup period.

Flexibility
One of the major components of our program is flexibility. Clients are able to:
• Specify I/E criteria
◦Only looking for females, or males, or a certain age, or even smokers or high
caffeine users, vaccine history etc
• Collection Specific Requirements: when preparing for your onsite, we identify which
tubes you would like us to provide so we are prepared for visits, but then give
researchers flexibility for those tube types if they want to switch from Sodium Heparin
or Citrate, Change the volume 10mL vs 100mL
• Recall specific donors or design longitudinal studies
◦For the majority of our onsite programs, we have about 80% retention for all of our
donors
◦We assign each donor a “PID” Patient Identification number, to make it easy for
you to call your project manager and say, I’d like to schedule another location from
PID 1234 or 5678; while still keeping that donor anonymous
• Manage all logistics & requests
◦Free up internal resources and leave all of the details to us
• Custom protocol allows you to design the program to meet all of your organization’s

End to End Management
Sanguine has been an experienced provider for onsite programs for many years.
• We handle the writing and submission of the IRB protocol, we can typically get this
program up and running within 6 weeks
• We ensure that our individual phlebotomists are certified. You can see here the
various certifications they go through to become a phlebotomist with Sanguine. We
even complete program specific training so they understand all of the requirements to
work within your organization
• We work with you to define specific days and times for phlebotomists to be at your
facility, ready to do draws so researchers can plan accordingly.
• We understand life happens and people may get sick, or a car might break down, so
we also have a backup phlebotomist in the area, and manage backup donors to try
and limit any reschedules so researchers always get their blood when they are
depending on it
• We have dedicated Research Consultants who handle scheduling and receiving
requests, repeat donor requests and manage the company specific onsite email
address, where someone on our end will be able to quickly answer and accommodate
flexibility for requests.
• We also provide you with a dedicated Project Manager to make sure the program is
running smoothly

Miscellaneous Questions we receive
• Can you confirm that Sanguine will not provide any financial statements or tax
documents at the end of the year to volunteers who have received $600+ in
financial compensation?
◦Yes, confirmed. At time of enrollment, Sanguine will ask employee to fill out a W-9
form for the tax year. If an employee declines, their donation amount will be
capped at $600. IRS code states that if a donor receives more than $600 in a
calendar, we must report this to the IRB under federal law.
• Do you have any details on the liabilities client incurs if a volunteer faints during
or after blood donation?
◦We have only had one person faint out of hundreds of collections <1% chance
◦We remind donors to drink water and have snacks pre and post apt
◦We can provide snacks and juice if requested from the client
◦Sanguine does not assume liability.
• Why do we need custom protocol?
◦If we used our umbrella protocol, they would qualify as a 'site' under the protocol
and would fall under the purview of Sanguine to audit them. Sanguine does not
want to audit them so they need their own protocol. Your response to the client

Conclusion
Employer on-site biospecimen collections offer convenient access to expedient
biospecimens from the workforce, benefiting scientific research efforts. This innovative
approach not only supports a wide range of research purposes but also creates a
meaningful sense of purpose for employees contributing to organizational research. The
establishment of on-site collection programs within companies provides a hassle-free,
cost-efficient, and compliant source of biospecimens, ultimately fostering advancements in
scientific goals.
