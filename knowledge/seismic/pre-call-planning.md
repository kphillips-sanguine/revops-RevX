Pre-call Planning is the preparation before a sales call to maximize the opportunity
Pre-call planning is an important step prior to a meeting. Pre-call planning should be done
for ALL meetings, as going in with a plan can help:
1. Increase confidence
2. Better manage time - you know what you need to accomplish in a certain period of time!
3. Improved outcomes - if you're prepared, you can better control the call and you're more
aligned on the objectives.

Research
We say this in so many trainings, but you should always research research research!
You do not want to spend too much time planning for your call - block 15-20 minutes to do
some preliminary research. Here's what you should focus on:
• Who is the call with? Investigate on LinkedIn and Salesforce.
◦What is their role?
◦How long have they been with the company?
◦Have we worked with them before? (Open opportunities at the company or even
past history)
• Company History
◦Are they a new client or existing?
◦What opportunities have we worked with them on?
◦What contracts do we currently have in place? CDA, MSA, MTA
◦What is their pipeline/alignment?
• Purpose of the call
◦Look at your notes from the SDR, inbound etc and understand what they want to
get out of this call
◦Put in ICs if necessary
◦Research things that are relevant: disease states, processing needs etc. and
familiarize their need to our product set

Preparing
Once your research is done, then what?
Start prepping what you want to get out of the call and plan out your ground rules. As a
reminder the ground rules should help you walk through the agenda of the call, including
what you want to get out of the call, what the client wants to get out of the call and next
steps if all goes well.
If your SDR set up the meeting, coordinate with them on the introduction plan and how to
have a seamless transition. That way, everyone is aligned on their role within the call.
You never know how a call is going to go as things can always take a turn but if you're
prepared and confident in your sales process, you should be able to pivot/
