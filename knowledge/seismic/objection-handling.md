Leukopak Objections
For Leukopaks in particular, we are getting push back on pricing. Some questions to ask
surrounding pricing are the following:
• Are we being compared apples to apples?
• We are noticing a lot of discrepancies where our competitors are NOT including certain
things we include such as IDF, CBC, ABO, demographics
• When these things are added on, we are actually LESS expensive (reference in Leuko
competitive)
• Whether the client is spending 200K with a competitor or 250K with us, either way you
look at it, it's still an investment. You will want to put doubt in their mind:
"We have heard from clients that they aren't receiving verification of diagnosis or
health records from all of their disease state donors. What happens if they get their
samples, theres not enough disease activity and they have to redo the sample
collections - that's wasted time, resources and money."
• Another way to phrase:
"For full transparency, a few of our clients have worked with us and the other vendors
and were not pleased when their samples from the other vendors arrived with no
clinical data and did not behave like "diseased samples" should behave in their
assays."
Data
• Competitors are not providing data like we are. This can add a lot of value to the
clients for the following reasons:
◦If there is variability in the leukopak, this is the best way to understand the WHY

Translational Objections
On the Translational side, we also meet many objections similar to Leukopaks focusing on
pricing and timelines.
Pricing
• One of the biggest pushbacks we get is when we try and explain the Patient Access
and Reservation costs - what goes into this cost?
◦Patient acquisition - identifying and screening these patients, disqualifying those
who don't meet criteria
◦Consenting patients into the study
◦Obtaining their medical records / ensuring they are redacted
◦Yes, we do have patients within our community, but depending on criteria we may
also need to find additional patients for the study
• "This is expensive."
◦This is a statement. I would reverse- "What are you comparing it to?"
◦As we discussed in the last section, we want to ensure we are being compared
apples to apples. For example:
▪Prospective vs Banked Inventory?
▪Do they include data? Many of our competitors do not ALWAYS include this
▪Verified diagnosis

Timeline
• "If you have all of these patients, why does it take so long?"
◦REVERSE FIRST! When do they need them by? Then focus on the reasons for
timelines....
▪VERIFY diagnosis
▪Updated Medical Records (this doesn't always occur)
▪Re-screen all of our patients to ensure they meet your criteria
• We are more conservative with our timelines because we want to ensure we deliver.
We often hear from clients where someone says they can get the samples in 3 months
and then 1 year goes by and no samples...
• Focus on deliverability and fulfillment rates and emphasize we can deliver
• Timelines are moving faster with our new EHR
