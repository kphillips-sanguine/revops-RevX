Asking Questions
How do you get to the P.I.E?
Ask better questions.
Asking open ended questions really helps getting the client to talk. It's your job to also
guide them down the right path.
If someone comes to you and explains all of their issues and what they are looking for, that
raises the red flag that they are going through this with multiple vendors and trying to get
down to the chase. This also tells me you are in the "Problem" stage and need to dig
deeper to find the impact.
• Oh wow, can you tell me more about that?
• How long have you been looking for these samples for? / How long has this project
been going on?
Be genuinely curious. Don't just ask questions to ask questions. Ask questions that flow,
that are relevant and can work their way down the funnel.

Pain Indicators
Pain indicators
Pain indicators are the surface level pains that prospects are comfortable telling you.
Here are examples of pain indicators:
• Poor sample quality
• Hard to reach population
• They want the option to do a longitudinal study
• They want the ability to recall specific donors
• They need samples collected a very specific way
• They’d like to have access to participant’s medical records
• Current providers keeps missing deadlines, not communicative
• They have very specific timelines to meet
While these are good places to start, if you start selling at this level you are positioning
yourself and Sanguine as a commodity. The determining factor when purchasing a

Business Impact
Business Impact
Business impact is all about how it impacts the team, project, timeline, budget, etc. It’s
important that you discuss the appropriate business impact at the appropriate level. If
you’re talking about long-term impacts to someone with a short term it isn’t going to
resonate.
Here are examples of business impacts:
• Waste money on samples that aren’t reliable
• Waste time doing the study only to start over
• Delays the entire study and the time it takes to get to clinical trials
• The clinical trial fails

Personal Impact
Personal Impact
Personal impact is where decisions are made, but it is also the most challenging level to
get to. If there are multiple people in the buying process you need to address their
individual personal impacts. The personal impact for a scientist is different from the
personal impact for procurement.
Here are examples of personal impacts:
• Not able to be confident in the data.
• Making decisions on inaccurate assumptions.
• Not hitting own deadlines for projects.
• Wasted time.
• Samples delayed → reschedule for the assay, working late in the lab to process
The personal impact shows their EMOTIONS about the problem.

Pain Best Practices
Pain Best Practices
• Pain is for them to get a clear understanding of their problem, potential problem, or
desire. Pain is not for you to use as a weapon against them.
• Keep the prospect talking by using softening statements the further you go down the
funnel of questions.
• Be mindful of your tone and body language as you’re asking more personal questions.
Your tone and body language should be different at the end of the funnel versus the
beginning.
• Ask one question at a time and give them a chance to answer.
• Genuinely be curious vs. asking leading questions.
• When the pain step is done well, you build rapport. When you don’t, you break rapport.
• Take notes so it’s easier to do your pain recap, and listen for emotionally charged
words like: concerned, uncertain, worried, overwhelmed, frustrated, etc.

Pain Questions
Pain Questions
• The purpose of this question is to get the prospect talking and to identify potential pain
indicators.
◦Can you walk me through that?
◦Can you elaborate?
◦How do you normally procure samples?
◦Why are you considering prospective collection?
◦What made you want to have a conversation today?
• This question starts giving you more clarity about what’s going on and it’s an easy
transition into business impact. Secondly, when people start sharing an example it
puts them back in the emotional state they were in (pain) when the problem initially
occurred.
◦Can you walk me through the last time that happened?
◦Can you give me an example?
◦Oh wow, what happened?
• You’re gauging their sense of urgency. This is particularly helpful when their pain is
another vendor.
◦How long have you been looking for ( insert what they’re looking for)?
◦How long does that typically take to get these samples?

Impact Questions
• If they’ve been trying low budget options it gives you an opportunity to contrast their
results with the results than can get with Sanguine, a premium option. It also gives you
an opportunity to identify additional opportunity costs and identify/explain why it didn’t
work.
◦How did that work out?
◦How did those samples work out?
• This is where you start helping them uncover other pain points and impacts they
weren’t aware of. Identifying the cost of their pain also gives you an anchor for the
budget step.
◦How does that impact (timeline, budget, reliability)?
▪Timeline is really important here
◦How does that impact your current study? Future study?
• You’re transitioning from business impact to personal impact.
◦That has to be (insert emotional word).
◦If we were able to do (insert what they want), what would be most helpful for you?
• You want them to start thinking about the best-case scenario and how Sanguine can
help them get there.
◦What were you hoping we could help you with?

Funnel
Treating your questions like a FUNNEL
When we start asking broader questions, this allows the client to have a more open ended
response, and then allow you to continue working down the funnel to find the route of the
problem.
Sometimes people start to low in the funnel or stop diving down into the funnel, which will
ultimately cause bottlenecks, or not give you the information you need.

Conclusion
If you don't know why the client wants to purchase from you, what problem they are going
to solve, what the impact is, you are likely not going to close the deal.
If you are able to master discovery, you are going to be able to build stronger rapport and
have a higher win rate within your opportunities.
Happy Selling!
