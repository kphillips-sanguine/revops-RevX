Other names of NPOs
When we are evaluating new or rare conditions, we turn to NPOs to help with recruitment
for these patients. Considering they have direct lines to patients and/or their caregivers,
they are able to reach these donors through a variety of methods:
• Newsletters
• Email blasts
• Social Media
• Website / Blog posts
• Etc.

Partnerships & Process
Sanguine has over 100+ partnerships with NPOs. These are listed next to the
condition in the Condition Summary for Feasibility Sheet.
In order to work with a NPO, they almost ALWAYS want to know who the research
organization is and the purpose of research for full transparency before they decide if this is
something they want to help with or not.

NPO Timeline
Working with an NPO can be a lengthy process. From initial check from the Sales Team,
outreach can take ~1 month. Sometimes NPOs respond instantly, others can take several
times, or don't respond at all.
Even after an NPO responds, the process follows:
• Initial meeting with NPO
• NDA signed
• Secondary meeting to discuss recruitment specifics

• Recruitment agreement signed
• Opportunity closed/won
• Recruitment content created, approved and submitted to IRB

Pricing
Most indications that require an NPO are rare diseases, so they would fall under a Tier 6.
There are some exceptions for the pricing:
1. The client referred us the NPO
a. Typically they handle the "donation" or payment to the NPO so we can lower or
discount the tier
2. If it's not a rare disease but we still need additional recruitment help, this will be tiered
as the normal prevalence as long as the tier pricing can cover the donation
3. We no longer charge a separate donation fee - this will be covered by the tier
4. We will need to charge an IRB fee for custom patient facing materials - not a custom
protocol though
