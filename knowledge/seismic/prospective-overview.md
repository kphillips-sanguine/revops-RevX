Prospective Overview
Sanguine is a pioneer of in-home specimen collection and a leader in direct-to-patient
recruitment and digital health innovation. We partner with pre-clinical researchers on their
study designs and support complex patient inclusion/exclusion sample collection criteria in
a PROSPECTIVE collection model. Sanguine can collect minimally invasive sample types
within Patients' Homes, which include, but not limited to:
• Blood
• Urine
• Saliva
• Stool
• Skin Taping
• Sputum
• Swabs
• Semen
These are designated by the
Prospective Opportunity Record Type in Salesforce
Prospective Collection
Prospective Collections consist of identifying and recruiting patients and then collecting one
or more types of biospecimens in the future.

Research Institutes
Research Institutes represent accounts that are involved in life science research, but are
not directly responsible for the commercialization or development of therapeutics.
• These accounts consist of Hospital Systems, Non-Profit Research orgs, & Universities,

can detect disease. Sequencing companies also fall under this record type
Diagnostic Companies are another net new account record type that do not make up the
traditional Sanguine customer

lower prices on a per patient basis and often don’t need access to stringent I/E criteria,
nor do they need a high sample volume per donor

Competitors
Direct Competitors:
• BioIVT
• Discovery Life Sciences (DLS)
• iSpecimen
There are many competitors in the space that have an in-direct patient model, which means
they do not have the direct relationship like we do. They are typically going through 3rd
parties such as physicians/clinics or partnerships.
Challenges our competitors face:
• Timeline is dependent on patients going to see the doctor, agreeing to enroll in the
study etc
◦More high risk patients avoiding dr apts in-person
◦Trouble for longitudinal studies if they cancel their apt/no show
◦Leads to unrealistic timelines provided to researchers
• Geographically bound / limits patient pools
• Lack of patient data (custom surveys, requested data points..)
• Challenges with recalling patients
• Poor customer service due to poor visibility into collections
• Can have various IRB/Consents across clinicians for the same study
Why clients use them:

In-Home Centrifugation
Centrifugation: The process of using a centrifuge to separate blood components through
spinning
Aliquot: Separate a portion of serum/plasma from the blood and place it in a separate tube
• Serum (⅓ blood volume)
◦Collected in SST tubes
◦Spinning Process:
◦
▪Let Clot for 30 minutes, Spin for 15, 2500rpm, Transfer by pipette
• Plasma (½ blood volume)
◦Collected in EDTA, Sodium Hep etc
◦Spinning Process:
▪Spin for 10 minutes, 2500rpm, Transfer by pipette
◦Can “Double Spin” for Platelet Rich Plasma (PRP - 20% yield from whole blood),
Platelet Poor Plasma (PPP - 30% yield from whole blood) if requested
• Urine
◦Separate Supernatant from Pellet = 1 spin

---

<!-- Page 22 -->
▪Note if they want to remove the pellet or not
Key details:
• Do not have refrigerated centrifuges, but we can put on ice to cool down, spin, aliquot
and freeze
• Standard is NOT spinning bucket, but we can purchase if it is a requirement
• Cannot accommodate 8mL CPT sodium citrate (would need to do a different size)
Maximum number of spins per visit
4
*remember, serum and plasma are also spun separately due to different processing
requirements
Minimum Aliquot Size within the home
1mL
*Anything smaller in the home will not be precise due to the pipettes and the nature of the
processing; We can do .5mL if they are OK that it will not be precise
